# Henry Labs — agentic commerce landscape notes

Filed June 10, 2026. Source: Granola notes from "Agentic commerce overview with
Armaan", May 21, 2026.

## Their model

- "Operating system for agentic commerce": embedded checkout for product
  aggregators — purchases complete inside discovery platforms (Pinterest,
  Instagram, chat) without redirects
- Saves payment/shipping after first purchase to boost conversion
- Value to platforms: detailed shopping data (color, size, price) they never
  had — previously only affiliate-commission-level signal ("something was
  bought"); enables retargeting
- Mechanism: agents acting like humans in browsers buy from merchants — **no
  direct merchant relationships needed**; mix of agent-based scraping and
  request-based catalog methods; reverse image search endpoint

## Market map (their view)

- Target customers: product aggregators — fashion discovery apps, cashback
  platforms (Rakuten/Honey type). Current clients: a fashion discovery app,
  price.com
- Fashion tech is the easiest segment; three discovery categories: reverse
  image search (My Aesthetic AI), AI closets/stylists (Get Me, Closet AI),
  secondhand aggregators (Retrofit, Benny)

## Relevance to Blackwell

Adjacent, not competing: Henry Labs serves aggregators on the demand side;
Blackwell works the merchant side (making brands legible/transactable to
agents). Their no-merchant-relationship browser-agent approach is a data point
for how agent traffic will hit merchant sites regardless of merchant readiness.
