Atlas is one of the Bay Area's most respected skate shops, an 19-year San Mateo institution that doubles as an art gallery. Its website does not tell Google, or any AI, that story. The shop is elite; the site's findability setup is not. Nearly every issue below is a fast, mechanical fix, not a rebuild.

The findings

In detail, worst first

Critical

1 · The homepage does not tell anyone what Atlas is

The title tag is the line Google shows in results and weighs most heavily for ranking. Atlas's is one word: Atlas. No "skate shop," no "San Mateo," no brands. The meta description is empty, so Google writes its own. There is no h1 on the page at all.

// atlasskateboarding.com, homepage <head>, today
<title>Atlas</title>
<meta name="description" content="">   // empty
<h1> ... </h1>   // none on the page
<meta property="og:image" content="http://atlasskateboarding.com/...">   // insecure scheme

Why it matters

You rank for "Atlas" and effectively nothing else, and "Atlas" is one of the most contested words on the web: an atlas, Atlas the Titan, Atlas Obscura, dozens of brands. Even "Atlas skate shop" is a fight the current setup is not equipped for. Searches like "skate shop San Mateo," "where to buy Antihero decks Bay Area," or "skate shop near me" never surface you. The empty description costs clicks on every link that does appear.

The fix

Descriptive, keyworded title and meta templates across the homepage, collections, and products; a real h1; and an https share image. Mechanical, theme-level, low-risk, measured in hours.

Critical

2 · 4,528 products carry no structured data

Product pages render no JSON-LD at all, the format Google uses for rich results (price, stock, star ratings) and the format AI shopping engines read to recommend and compare products. The data exists in Shopify, real SKUs and real barcodes. It is just never published where machines look.

// Any Atlas product page, structured data found
JSON-LD Product schema:   none
Offer / price / availability in schema:   none
AggregateRating / Review:   none

// CCS, Tactics, and Labor all publish full Product + Offer schema.

Why it matters

No rich results in Google, so your listings are plain blue links while competitors show price, stock, and stars. And when someone asks ChatGPT, Perplexity, or Google's AI "where can I buy X," Atlas cannot be quoted, because there is nothing structured to quote. Across 4,528 products, nearly double the DLX catalog we looked at last week, that is a lot of invisible inventory.

The fix

Emit Product + Offer schema (price, availability, SKU, brand) across the catalog from data Shopify already holds, plus AggregateRating where reviews exist. One template change, whole catalog covered.

High

3 · No machine-readable identity, and "Atlas" is highly ambiguous

There is no Organization or LocalBusiness schema, so search and AI have no structured idea of what Atlas is, that it is a physical San Mateo shop and gallery, or that it has run since 2007. The name alone collides with an enormous field. The homepage is a product grid with no brand narrative, even though the about page tells an excellent story.

From the Atlas about page: the shop opened in May 2007 in downtown San Mateo, in the San Francisco Bay Area, and runs as a retail store and art gallery, "driven by passion over profit." That sentence lives on the site. No machine can read it as identity.

Why it matters

"What is Atlas?" / "is Atlas legit?" / "Atlas skate shop hours" have no authoritative machine-readable answer, so AI guesses or stays silent. For a shop whose whole edge is credibility, curation, and an 19-year scene, having no entity is the difference between being the answer and being absent.

The fix

Organization + LocalBusiness schema (location, hours, sameAs to socials), a short brand block on the homepage, and entity groundwork so "Atlas" resolves to the shop, not a titan or an atlas.

High

4 · The AI-commerce layer is switched on but empty

Shopify auto-provisions an agent stack: llms.txt, agents.md, a UCP/MCP endpoint, an agentic-discovery sitemap. Atlas has all of it, and the UCP transaction endpoint genuinely works. But the llms.txt and agents.md are the generic Shopify boilerplate (how to transact), not a content map (who you are, what you carry, why you). Every Shopify store ships this identical file.

llms.txt and agents.md = the default "Agent Instructions" template (about 4.4 KB) routing agents to Shop Pay. Zero brand, category, or curation content. This is exactly the gap we specialize in closing.

Why it matters

AI shopping is moving to live retrieval. To an agent reading your llms.txt today, Atlas is an anonymous Shopify store, not "the San Mateo shop and gallery with one of the deepest Antihero, Krooked, and independent-brand selections in the Bay." That framing is won or lost in the content layer, and right now it is blank.

The fix

Rewrite llms.txt and agents.md as a real content map (brand, categories, signature brands, the San Mateo store and gallery, what makes the curation distinct), keeping the working Shopify transaction layer underneath.

Medium

5 · Real assets, badly under-leveraged

Atlas has assets most shops would envy: 4,528 products across dozens of brands, 106 collections, and an unusually deep editorial archive of roughly 990 posts across three blogs (news, projects, videos), including artist series and collaboration features with Alien Workshop, Lakai, Stussy, and Antihero. But the blog carries no Article schema and is not wired into SEO, and the catalog churns constantly (drops, restocks, footwear size runs), which appears to be managed by hand.

A note we would rather raise in person than assume: a lot of the day-to-day around a catalog this size, onboarding new arrivals from distributor feeds, posting restocks and drops, answering "is this in my size," cross-posting to social, tends to be repetitive, manual work. We do not want to presume how Atlas runs; we would want to understand your week first. But this is usually where, after the findability fixes land, a second and cheap layer is worth scoping: automating the busywork.

Traffic & paid acquisition

Before the Meta ads, get the plumbing ready

You mentioned wanting help with traffic, including Meta ads, beyond SEO and GEO. The good news is that the fixes above are what make paid traffic pay off. The caution is that the Meta measurement plumbing is not connected yet, so spend today would fly partly blind.

What we can see from the storefront

Google Ads (conversion tag AW-841187191) and GA4 (G-3PF1CHM497) are live, and email (Klaviyo) and SMS (Attentive) are connected. But no Meta pixel fires on the storefront. Shopify's customer-events configuration shows the Meta app slot present with its system-user token empty, so neither the browser pixel nor the Conversions API is sending events to Meta. Tactics, by contrast, runs a live Meta pixel today.

Why this matters for the Meta plan

Advantage+ Shopping and dynamic product ads, the formats that make Meta efficient for a 4,500-product catalog, run on a clean product feed. That feed is the same structured product data that finding 2 says is missing. Fix the catalog data once and you feed Google Shopping, AI shopping, and Meta's catalog at the same time. Without the pixel and the Conversions API, you cannot measure cost per purchase or build retargeting and lookalike audiences, and with current iOS signal loss the server-side Conversions API is close to mandatory. Cold paid clicks also land on collection and product pages, which need the titles, descriptions, and speed the on-page fixes deliver, or you pay for visits that bounce.
