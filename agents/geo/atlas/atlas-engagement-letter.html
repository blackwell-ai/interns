Background

Shoppers find skate shops through Google search and, increasingly, AI answer engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Google AI Mode). The same machine-readable gaps hurt both: a one-word title, empty metadata, and missing structured data keep a shop out of Google's rich results and out of the AI answers alike. The visibility audit Blackwell delivered separately on June 16, 2026 put Atlas at 44 of 100 (F): absent from "best Bay Area skate shop" results that named competitors, 4,528 products with no structured data, and a real 4.8-star reputation no machine can connect to the storefront. This engagement closes those gaps across both surfaces.

Engagement and scope

The 30-day implementation sprint that closes the gaps the audit identified:

  1. On-page and entity foundation. Descriptive title, meta, and h1 templates across homepage, collections, and products; an https share image; and Organization plus LocalBusiness schema for the San Mateo store and gallery, with sameAs links connecting the site to the Yelp, Google, Nike SB, and social profiles that hold the 4.8-star reputation.
  2. Catalog structured data. Product and Offer schema (price, availability, SKU, brand) across all 4,528 products, with AggregateRating where reviews exist, exposed for both Google rich results and AI shopping engines.
  3. Agent-commerce content layer. llms.txt and agents.md rewritten as a real content map (signature brands, categories, the San Mateo shop) with agent-readable pricing, over the working Shopify transaction layer; Article schema and internal linking across the blog archive.
  4. Paid-acquisition readiness. Meta pixel and Conversions API connected, and the product catalog feed prepared for Advantage+ and dynamic product ads.
  5. Benchmark setup and verification. Joint benchmarks set at kickoff and re-measured at day 30: composite visibility score, schema-validator pass rate and Google rich-result coverage across the catalog, presence in AI category answers for "skate shop San Mateo" and "Peninsula skate shop," and Meta event match quality. Schema-validator pass at 100% required for sign-off.

What we need from Atlas

  1. A single point of contact with authority to approve site changes and answer questions within one business day.
  2. Shopify theme deploy access (or a designated Atlas developer paired with Blackwell), read-only admin access for the engagement, and Meta Business access to connect the pixel and Conversions API.
  3. Openness to a few early calls: which signature brands and categories to foreground in the content map, how to represent the San Mateo store and gallery as the entity, and confirmation of the review and social profiles to link. We bring recommendations; the calls are Atlas's.

Continued work

The 30-day engagement is Phase 1. Continued work, scoped after benchmarks land, covers ongoing search and AI visibility (per-dimension monitoring, entity establishment so "Atlas" resolves to the shop, and placement in the category sources AI engines cite) and protocol-evolution work as agent-commerce standards advance. Separately and at no additional cost, Blackwell will explore an AI assistant pilot with Atlas as design partner: a tool that takes repetitive catalog work off the team (new-arrival onboarding, restock and drop posts, stock-question replies, social cross-posting). Any full version is scoped and priced on its own.
