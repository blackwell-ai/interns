Executive summary

An elite shop with broken findability plumbing

Atlas is one of the Bay Area's most respected skate shops, a San Mateo institution and art gallery open since 2007. Its website does not tell Google, or any AI, that story. The five issues below are fast, mechanical fixes, not a rebuild, and they are the same gaps we found on DLX last week, here at nearly double the scale: 4,528 products with no machine-readable data behind them.

The findings, worst first

Five fixes, mostly mechanical

Critical

1. The homepage does not say what Atlas is

The title tag, the line Google weighs most, is the one word "Atlas." The meta description is empty, there is no h1, and the share image is declared over insecure http://. You rank for "Atlas," one of the most contested words on the web, and little else. "Skate shop San Mateo" and "Antihero decks near me" never surface you.

<title>Atlas</title> · meta description: empty · h1 tags: 0 · og:image over http://

Fix. Descriptive title, meta, and h1 templates across homepage, collections, and products, plus an https share image. Theme-level, measured in hours.

Critical

2. 4,528 products carry no structured data

No product page renders any JSON-LD, the format Google uses for rich results (price, stock, stars) and the format AI shopping engines read to recommend and compare. The data sits in Shopify; it is never exposed. So your listings are plain blue links, and when a shopper asks ChatGPT or Google AI "where can I buy X," Atlas cannot be quoted. CCS, Tactics, and Labor expose all of it.

Product / Offer / AggregateRating schema on a product page: none

Fix. Emit Product + Offer schema (price, availability, SKU, brand) across the catalog from data Shopify already holds. One template, all 4,528 products covered.

High

3. No machine-readable identity, and "Atlas" is ambiguous

There is no Organization or LocalBusiness schema, so search and AI do not know Atlas is a physical San Mateo shop and gallery open since 2007. The about page tells the story ("driven by passion over profit"); no machine can read it. "What is Atlas / is Atlas legit / Atlas hours" get a guess or silence.

Homepage JSON-LD blocks: 0 (no entity, no LocalBusiness, no store info)

Fix. Organization + LocalBusiness schema (location, hours, socials), a short homepage brand block, and entity groundwork so "Atlas" resolves to the shop.

High

4. The AI-commerce layer is switched on but empty

Shopify auto-provisions llms.txt, agents.md, a working UCP/MCP endpoint, and an agentic-discovery sitemap. Atlas has all of it, but the llms.txt is the generic 4.4 KB Shopify boilerplate, not a content map. To an agent reading it today, Atlas is an anonymous Shopify store, not the Bay Area shop with one of the deepest Antihero and independent-brand selections.

llms.txt = default "Agent Instructions" template, ~4.4 KB, zero brand or curation content

Fix. Rewrite llms.txt and agents.md as a real content map (brands, categories, the San Mateo store and gallery), keeping the working transaction layer underneath.

Medium

5. Real assets, badly under-leveraged

Atlas has assets most shops would envy: 4,528 products, 106 collections, and roughly 990 posts across three blogs (news, projects, videos), including collaboration features with Alien Workshop, Lakai, Stussy, and Antihero. The blog carries no Article schema and is not wired into SEO, and the catalog churns by hand.

Fix. Article schema and SEO wiring on the blog archive now. Later, and only if it is worth it, scope automating the catalog busywork (new-arrival onboarding, restock and drop alerts), after we understand how you run things.

Traffic & paid acquisition

Before the Meta ads, get the plumbing ready

You want help with traffic, including Meta ads, beyond SEO and GEO. The fixes above are what make paid pay off, and the Meta measurement plumbing is not connected yet, so spend today would fly partly blind.

Google Ads (AW-841187191) and GA4 (G-3PF1CHM497) are live, with Klaviyo and Attentive connected. But no Meta pixel fires on the storefront; Shopify's customer-events config shows the Meta app slot present with its system-user token empty, so neither the browser pixel nor the Conversions API is sending events. Tactics, by contrast, runs a live Meta pixel. Advantage+ and dynamic product ads, the formats that make Meta efficient for a 4,500-product catalog, also run on a clean product feed, which is the same structured data finding 2 is missing. Fix the catalog once and you feed Google Shopping, AI shopping, and Meta together.
