Gaps we will close, by impact

Priority 1No product identifiers (sku, gtin, mpn)

The Product node has no GTIN, UPC, or SKU. GTIN is the key an engine uses to know your page and the Amazon, Ulta, or Target listing are the same product. Without it, engines cannot confidently match your page to the retailer listing, which is why they fell back to retailer prices in the audit. Adding GTINs is what lets your own page win the price.

Priority 2Size and variant relationships are not modeled

Each size is its own page with a single Offer. There is no ProductGroup tying the sizes together, so an engine on the 30ml page has no signal that other sizes exist or what they cost. This is the residual of the 12ml versus 30ml price confusion.

Priority 2Offer is missing commerce fields you already have

The Offer has no shipping details, return policy, price-valid-until, or offer URL. Your llms.txt already states the shipping tiers and you have a return policy. Putting them in OfferShippingDetails and MerchantReturnPolicy completes the offer for agentic checkout and Merchant-style listings.

Priority 3Aggregate ratings only, no review text

You expose the aggregate rating but no individual Review objects, though you have thousands of reviews. Engines quote review text in answers, so surfacing a sample feeds how they describe the product.

Priority 3Thin homepage entity schema

The homepage carries only an OnlineStore node. There is no sameAs linking your social and retailer profiles, no contactPoint, and no WebSite search action. sameAs is how an engine grounds “Good Molecules” as one confident entity. Low effort, high value.

Priority 3No breadcrumbs, no collection lists, thin social meta, missing files

No BreadcrumbList on product pages, no ItemList on category pages, and only og:site_name for social meta. Separately, sitemap.xml returns 404, llms-full.txt, agents.md, and the /.well-known/ endpoints are still absent, and there is no FAQPage markup or on-page FAQ.
