Re-checked live, June 11

CriticalAn agent still cannot buy

The agent endpoints (/.well-known/ucp, agent.json, ai-plugin.json) return 403. The product pages have no checkout: “Add To Cart” exists only as a script string next to “Not Available Online,” and “Buy Now” is financing copy. The price ($2,599.99 / $3,299.99 / $4,299.99) appears only in the structured-data block, not in the visible text.

CriticalThe quotable reputation numbers got worse

Trustpilot, read this morning: 1.4 of 5 across 872 reviews, 81% one-star, profile still unclaimed. Trustpilot’s sidebar on that page advertises Mammotion (4.0, 2.6K reviews) and Navimow. A June 9 review reads “Bought 3 robot lawn mowers… all 3 died.”

BBB, re-pulled on the entity registered to husqvarna.com/us (Husqvarna Professional Products, Inc., Charlotte NC): A+ letter rating, 1.03 of 5 customer average across 58 reviews, 204 complaints in three years. The corrected entity reads slightly worse than the June 2 figures (1.04, 191).

HighDiscontinued models still look live to a machine

The 430X URL now redirects to a support page marked discontinued, but that page still serves a Product schema node, and the 450X and 450XH URLs still return 200. This is why engines answer brand queries with the wrong, costlier model, and why the 430X shows up in Rufus results.

HighThe pages don’t answer the question buyers actually ask

The top objection in the third-party corpus is satellite dropout under trees. The product pages say nothing about trees or canopy, in the text or in any of the nine structured FAQs, even though the iQ’s wire fallback answers it directly. The offer markup also omits GTIN, rating, reviews, shipping, returns and seller, and the description field is empty.

StrengthAccess is clean and recent fixes are real

Every AI crawler we tested gets a byte-identical page. The pages now carry FAQ schema that opens with the wire-free answer, captioned imagery and video markup, all server-rendered. The remaining work is specific and small; Phase 1 specifies it.

Rufus reads Amazon’s catalog, and it gets the iQ right: wire-free in every answer, with the 430X correctly flagged as the wired one. The open-web engines read the third-party corpus and call the same products wired. That contrast localizes the problem. The product data is fine; the corpus is not.

Amazon listings also run $38 over the brand site on the 410 and 420, a third-party seller signature.

Where “wired and dated” comes from

We swept 18 ranking pages, the comparison sites, Reddit and YouTube. The pattern: major publishers are still testing 2022 wired models, and the best-ranked wire-free content is written by competitors. Engines repeat what they can retrieve.

Only 4 of the 18 ranking pages mention the iQ at all, all small blogs, and those are the amplification targets. Why this matters: 43.8% of the pages ChatGPT cites on “best X” questions are exactly these lists, 79% updated within a year (Ahrefs, 26K cited URLs), and YouTube mentions are the strongest measured correlate of AI visibility (r ≈ 0.74, Ahrefs, 75K brands). ChatGPT’s citations track Bing, not Google, and about 90% of what it cites sits past position 20 in Google, so existing SEO work doesn’t transfer.

  • A patch spec for each iQ page: ready-to-paste structured data (rating from the real ~4.0 retailer reviews, GTIN, description, shipping, returns, seller), the validator fix list, FAQ copy answering the under-trees objection, and a visible-price recommendation.
