Stream2SeaAI visibility

How Stream2Sea shows up when people ask AI assistants about sunscreen, and what we would do about it in 30 days.

What we found

Our audit scored the site 69 out of 100. The technical setup is good: AI crawlers can read the site, the product data is structured, and the checkout works for AI shopping agents. The gap is reputation and recommendations. When ChatGPT or Claude answer from memory, they recommend Stream2Sea. When the engines search the web live, they mostly drop it and name Project Reef and Badger instead, because those brands own the reef-safe sunscreen guides that engines quote, like Wirecutter, Travel and Leisure, and Good Housekeeping.

What we would do

  1. Get Stream2Sea into the reef-safe guides the engines quote (Wirecutter, Treeline Review, Travel and Leisure, Good Housekeeping). This is the one that matters most.
  2. Put the Amazon star ratings and review counts into the site's own product data, so engines quote Stream2Sea's ratings instead of falling back to Amazon.
  3. Link the homepage to the Amazon store, Trustpilot, and social profiles so engines read them as one brand.
  4. Fix the homepage title so it loads in the page source, not only after JavaScript runs. Right now an AI crawler sees no title.
  5. Add product IDs (GTIN and SKU) to the product data so engines do not swap in a retailer's price by mistake.
  6. Get the Protect Land and Sea certification that Badger leads with, and show it on the site.
  7. Fill out Trustpilot (2 reviews today), open a BBB profile, and get more mentions on Reddit and YouTube.
  8. Rewrite the llms.txt file, which is a generic 4KB Shopify default today, with the real catalog and positioning.

How we would measure it (30 days, money back)

We set the baseline on day one by asking the six main AI engines the sunscreen questions and recording the answers. We check again at day 30. The targets we agree on:

  • Stream2Sea named in more of the live answers. Today it shows up in about two of the six engines.
  • The data fixes live and passing a schema checker: the profile links, the ratings, and a working title.
  • Trustpilot reviews up from 2, and a BBB profile live.

If we miss the targets we agree on, you get the $1,000 back.
