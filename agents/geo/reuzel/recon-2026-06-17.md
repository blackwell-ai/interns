# Reuzel recon and frozen truth table, June 17, 2026

Internal working notes for the AI visibility audit of reuzel.com. Methodology:
`brain/company/audit-methodology.md`. Passive recon and the truth-table freeze ran
first, before any engine query, per the charter. Live engine testing (the two-pass
prompt battery) and the reputation corpus are the next phase and still open.

All checks run June 17, 2026 from curl with named user-agents. Apex (reuzel.com)
301-redirects to www, so www is canonical. Platform is Shopify (`powered-by:
Shopify`, `shopify-complexity-score` headers, `_shopify_essential` cookie, backing
store `reuzelinc.myshopify.com`), fronted by Cloudflare.

## Headline: this is the inverse of Good Molecules and HUM on agentic readiness

Good Molecules blocked the AI assistants at the WAF and shipped malformed schema.
HUM served clean schema but had no agentic-commerce surfaces at all. Reuzel, on
Shopify, ships the full agentic stack live and lets every AI crawler in. The
platform did the hard infrastructure work for free. What is left to win is not
crawlability or agent-readiness, it is the things Shopify does not hand you:
the brand entity is mislabeled in the schema, the review corpus is invisible to
non-JavaScript crawlers, and the catalog metadata is half-empty. Those are the
findings, and they all sit on dimensions Shopify does not auto-solve
(Discoverability, Quotability, Reputation).

## Structured data (PDP: /products/pink-pomade-heavy-hold-medium-shine, raw HTML, ClaudeBot UA)

Four JSON-LD blocks in server-rendered source, all parse:

- **Product** — name "Pink Strong Hold Grease Pomade", sku REU203, with a nested
  Offer (price 19.95, priceCurrency USD, availability InStock, variant URL). Server
  rendered, so a non-JavaScript crawler reads price and stock. This is a strength.
- **Two Organization blocks**, both name "Reuzel", url www.reuzel.com, with sameAs
  to Facebook, Instagram, YouTube, Twitter.
- **FAQPage** block present.

Three findings sit inside the schema, none of which Shopify fixes for you:

1. **Brand is mislabeled.** The Product schema's `brand` is
   `{"@type":"Brand","name":"reuzelinc"}` — the myshopify store slug, not "Reuzel".
   The string "reuzelinc" appears 39 times in the page source. The Organization
   blocks say "Reuzel" correctly, so the page contradicts itself on the brand
   entity. An engine that reads `Product.brand` to attribute the product gets
   "reuzelinc", which does not match how anyone refers to the brand. This is an
   entity-resolution risk on Discoverability and Recommendability.

2. **No aggregateRating in the schema, despite four review apps loaded.** The
   Product block carries no `aggregateRating` and no `review` nodes. Yet the page
   loads Okendo (17 references), Yotpo (20), Loox (6), and Judge.me (1) client-side.
   The ratings and review counts render in JavaScript widgets, so a non-JS crawler
   sees zero reviews in structured form. The social proof exists for human visitors
   and is invisible to the engines that read raw HTML. This is the central
   Quotability and Reputation finding, and it is fixable (emit AggregateRating into
   the server-rendered Product JSON-LD from the live review source).

3. **Review-app bloat.** Four separate review/UGC apps are referenced on one PDP.
   Only one is plausibly the live source of truth; the rest are likely leftover
   from migrations. Beyond page weight, it muddies which review corpus is canonical
   and is worth confirming with the customer.

Minor: the Product block uses `@context: http://schema.org/` (http, not https).
Google tolerates it; strict validators prefer https. The two Organization blocks
also carry duplicated and malformed sameAs entries (empty strings, a bare `#`).

OpenGraph and product meta are present in source (og:title, og:description,
og:image, meta description with the full product pitch).

## Catalog and frozen truth table

Full catalog pulled from /products.json (4 pages, limit 250). Saved to
`truth-table-raw.csv` (89 products, 130 variants).

- **Price range $2.00 to $800.00.** The $800 line is a Reuzel Academy class, not a
  retail SKU; retail products cluster around $11 to $23 for the styling line.
- **Flagship pomade line** (the grease and water-based pomades the brand is known
  for): Pink Strong Hold Grease $19.95, Blue High Shine $19.95, Red Water Soluble
  High Shine $19.95, Green Grease $19.95, Fiber $20.95, Clay Matte $20.95, Extreme
  Hold Matte $20.95, Concrete Hold Matte $20.95 (out of stock), plus a REUZEL x
  Liquid Death Severed Head Matte Clay $22.95 collab.
- **Availability:** 118 of 130 variants in stock. Six products fully out of stock,
  most of them date-bound Reuzel Academy classes and posters; the one real retail
  OOS is Concrete Hold Matte Pomade. Stock is healthy.
- **Metadata gap:** 49 of 89 products carry an empty `product_type`. The rest are
  typed (Pomade 10, Beard Products 8, Reuzel Academy 6, Hair Tonic 4, Tattoo
  Aftercare 4, Shampoo 3, plus singles). Empty product_type weakens faceted
  discovery and the category signal an engine can read from the feed. Worth fixing
  in the catalog.

The catalog is well-priced, in stock, and clean. The truth table is frozen here for
grading; engine answers captured in phase 2 are graded against this file, never the
other way around.

## What is still open (phase 2, needs /browse and human assist)

- **Two-pass prompt battery** (browsing off, then forced on) across the six engines:
  ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Gemini, Google AI Mode/AI Overviews, Microsoft
  Copilot. Capture whether each cites reuzel.com, gets price and hold/finish right,
  and names Reuzel in category queries (best pomade for men, strong hold pomade,
  water-based vs oil-based pomade, best matte clay) against Suavecito, Layrite,
  Baxter of California, American Crew, Imperial, and similar. Reuzel is a strong
  retail brand, so the question is whether that translates into engine
  recommendations, not whether it gets crawled.
- **Does the absent aggregateRating actually cost citations?** Check whether engines
  that read raw HTML report "no rating data" or pull the rating from a third party
  (Amazon, Sephora, Ulta, Sally Beauty) instead of the brand's own site.
- **Reputation corpus:** Reddit (r/wicked_edge, r/malegrooming, pomade communities),
  YouTube (the brand and Schorem barbershop have a large following), Amazon and
  retailer review bodies, Trustpilot. Reuzel's barbershop heritage (Schorem,
  Rotterdam) is an entity-strength asset worth confirming engines associate with it.
- **Confirm which review app is canonical** and pull the real aggregate rating and
  review count per flagship SKU, so the proposal can show the exact AggregateRating
  payload to inject.
- Grade the five dimensions against this frozen truth table, then build the deck.
