### Where to meet merchants in person (next 3 weeks)

The recurring markets below give you a vendor-owner to talk to almost any day with
no RSVP. Lead with those for volume of conversations; use the Shopify and seller
meetups for owners already in a networking mindset.

San Francisco (Mission first):

- Mission Community Market. Thursdays 3 to 7pm (Jun 18, 25; Jul 2), 22nd St
  between Mission and Valencia. Farmers plus local small businesses.
  https://foodwise.org/markets/mission-community-market/
- Off the Grid Night Market, Fort Mason. Fri Jun 19, 5 to 10pm. Around 25 food
  founders plus 50+ makers (West Coast Craft). Dense founder mix.
  https://fortmason.org/series/fort-mason-night-market-2026/
- Castro Night Market. Fri Jun 19, 5 to 9pm.
  https://sf.funcheap.com/city-guide/night-market-san-francisco-bay-area/
- Secondhand Saturday (Sunset Mercantile). Sat Jun 21, 60+ vintage and maker
  vendors. https://sunsetmercantilesf.com/innersunsetflea/
- Downtown First Thursdays (SoMa). Thu Jul 2, 5 to 10pm, large maker and retail
  street market.
- Ferry Plaza Farmers Market. Sat, Tue, Thu year-round, very high vendor count.
  https://foodwise.org/markets/ferry-plaza-farmers-market/
- Also any day: Heart of the City (Wed and Sun, Civic Center); Alemany Farmers
  Market (Sat) and Alemany Flea (Sun), Bernal Heights.

South Bay:

- Downtown San Jose Farmers' Market (San Pedro Square). Fridays 10am to 2pm (Jun
  19, 26; Jul 3), plus a Wednesday market at the Paseo.
  https://sanpedrosquare.org/downtown-farmers-market/
- San José Made Prismatic Market. Sat Jun 20, 2 to 7pm, downtown San Jose, drawn
  from a 250+ maker community. https://www.sanjosemade.com/pages/events

On-target meetups (RSVP early):

- Shopify San Francisco Community. https://www.meetup.com/Shopify-San-Francisco-Community/
- Bay Area Amazon and Shopify Sellers. https://www.meetup.com/bay_area_amazon_shopify_sellers/

### Target shops to approach (Mission-first, fits the converting profile)

The profile that has converted is a younger, open-minded, direct owner, often
running more than one venture and receptive to AI (the skate-shop archetype).

Top 5:

1. State of Flux, 1176 Valencia St, Mission. Streetwear shop with in-house print
   and photo studio, two young design-savvy co-founders, Shopify, 35K Instagram.
   Closest to the archetype. https://www.stateofflux.shop (@stateoffluxshop)
2. Low Key Skate Shop, 679 Geary St. Skate shop plus art gallery, screen-prints
   its own apparel, monthly art shows, Shopify.
   https://www.lowkeysanfrancisco.com (@lowkeysanfrancisco)
3. Nooworks, 395 Valencia St. Woman-owned artist-collaboration apparel, grown
   from an apartment operation to a Valencia flagship, Shopify.
   https://nooworks.com (@nooworks)
4. Arcana, 2512 Mission St. Plant boutique plus natural wine bar plus event
   venue, genuinely multi-venture owner, 20K Instagram.
   https://www.arcanasf.com (@arcanasf)
5. Abanico Coffee Roasters, 2121 Mission St. Owner-operated specialty roaster
   with an online bean shop. https://www.abanicocoffee.com (@abanicocoffeeroasters)

Also worth a walk-in or DM:

- Mission Skateboards, 3045 24th St. Core skate shop, light web presence, so a
  likely GEO and online-visibility need. https://missionsk8shop.com
- Earthen Shop, 142 Fillmore St, Lower Haight. Woman-owned ceramics and homeware
  maker, Shopify. https://earthen-shop.com
- impAvid, Oakland. Young female founder, quarterly Shopify drops; best as a warm
  DM. https://impavidoakland.com (@impavidoakland)
- Circle-A Skateboards, 380 S First St, San Jose. Skate plus coffee,
  multi-venture, 12K Instagram. https://www.circlea.com

Pre-qualify fast with the "Powered by Shopify" footer check and the Valencia
Corridor Merchants directory (https://www.visitvalenciastreet.com); walking
Valencia from 16th to 24th and 24th St covers most of the list.
