# Launch and marketing plan

The story is the distribution. An apparel brand designed and run by an autonomous agent is a thing people forward, which is the cheapest acquisition there is. Lead with the story, let the clothes hold it up.

## Phase 0, before launch (no spend)

- Build the store on the copy and designs in this repo.
- Order one sample of each SKU, inspect print and fit, photograph on flat ground in the brand palette.
- Stand up the brand accounts (handle convention: @annealsupply or @wearanneal, confirm availability at setup).
- Write the launch note: the honest version of "a machine made a clothing brand, here is the first drop."

## Phase 1, founder-network launch (no paid spend)

The first hundred buyers should come from the founder network and the AI and builder crowd that finds the premise interesting. Channels:

- A founder post telling the real story, made-by-an-agent, with the drop. This audience overlaps exactly with the people most likely to find it interesting.
- The build-in-public angle: the agent operating the brand is itself the content. Show the curve, the reasoning, the unit economics. Transparency is on-brand and it is true.
- Email the warm list with the launch note.

Goal of phase 1 is signal, not scale: does anyone outside our own network actually buy, and which SKU.

## Phase 2, paid test (capped)

Only if phase 1 shows conversion. One channel, hard cap of $10 per day, creative that leads with the premise and shows the product cleanly. Measure blended customer acquisition cost against the thresholds in `ops/financials.md` (under ~$25 on the tee, under ~$45 on the hoodie). Kill it fast if it does not clear.

## Phase 3, retention

- A simple welcome email flow and an abandoned-checkout flow in Klaviyo, both written in brand voice.
- Drops as the rhythm: limited, infrequent, each with one new design and a clear reason to exist. Scarcity that is real, not manufactured.

## Content engine

The brand can produce its own content because the operator is the story. Recurring formats:
- The reasoning behind each design (the curve, the palette logic, why this blank).
- Plain-spoken numbers: what a tee costs to make, what we keep, why the price is the price.
- The schematic motif as endlessly remixable visual content.
