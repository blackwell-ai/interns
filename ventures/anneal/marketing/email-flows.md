# Email and SMS flows, ready to load into Klaviyo

Brand voice throughout: calm, exact, no hype, no em or en dashes. These are the three flows that matter at launch. Subject lines are written to be opened by a real person, not a marketer.

## Flow 1: welcome (triggers on signup)

Email 1, sent immediately.
- Subject: You are early to Anneal
- Body:
  Thanks for signing up. Quick context, since this brand is a little unusual.
  Anneal is clothing designed and run end to end by an autonomous agent. Every piece is made only after you order it, so nothing is wasted and every size stays in stock.
  The first drop is three pieces built around one idea, the anneal curve, the heat-hold-cool line the brand is named for. Have a look.
  Button: See the drop

Email 2, sent two days later (only if no purchase).
- Subject: Why a machine made you a t-shirt
- Body:
  The honest version. Annealing is a process: heat something, then cool it slowly, and it comes out stronger and clearer. That is how this brand is made, a patient system instead of a loud one.
  Made on order means full size range, always, and a price set by good blanks and good printing rather than a markup on a logo.
  Button: See the drop

## Flow 2: abandoned checkout (triggers 1 hour after checkout started, not completed)

Email 1, after 1 hour.
- Subject: Your Anneal order is still open
- Body:
  You left something in your cart. It is still here. Because every piece is made on order, nothing is reserved until you check out, so finish when you are ready.
  Button: Finish checkout

Email 2, after 23 hours (only if still not purchased).
- Subject: Last note on your cart
- Body:
  Still holding your cart. One thing worth knowing: exchanges are limited because each piece is made for you, so the size guide on each product is accurate to the blank. If you were unsure on size, that is the place to check.
  Button: Finish checkout

Optional SMS (only if they opted in), after 4 hours:
  Your Anneal cart is still open. Made on order, so finish whenever. [link]

## Flow 3: post-purchase (triggers on order placed)

Email 1, immediately (order confirmation supplements Shopify's).
- Subject: We are making your order
- Body:
  Order received. Here is what happens now: your piece gets printed on order, which takes two to four business days, then it ships and you get tracking. Total is usually three to five business days in the US.
  Nothing is sitting in a warehouse, it is being made for you. That is the tradeoff for no waste and every size in stock.

Email 2, sent 5 days after delivery.
- Subject: How did it come out
- Body:
  Your Anneal piece should have landed. Two asks. If anything is off, a misprint or a flaw, reply with a photo and we reprint or refund, your choice, no need to send it back. If it came out right, the best thing you can do for a brand run by a machine is tell one person about it.
  Thanks for being early.

## Notes for setup

- Keep all of these as plain, near-text emails, minimal images, on-brand restraint. No countdown timers, no "ONLY 2 LEFT" since made-on-order makes that a lie.
- SMS only to opted-in contacts, and keep it to the abandoned-checkout nudge at launch.
