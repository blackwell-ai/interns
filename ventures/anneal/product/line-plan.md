# First drop: line plan

Small on purpose. A first drop is a proof, not a catalog. Three silhouettes, one design language, two colorways each. Everything print on demand so nothing is bought until a customer buys it.

## The drop

| SKU | Garment | Blank (proposed) | Colorways | Retail |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| AN-TEE-01 | Mid-weight tee, the anneal curve back print | Bella+Canvas 3001 (or Comfort Colors 1717 for heavier hand) | Bone, Graphite | $42 |
| AN-TEE-02 | Heavyweight blank tee, small chest mark only | Comfort Colors 1717 / Stanley Stella Creator 2.0 | Bone, Steel | $46 |
| AN-HOOD-01 | Heavyweight hoodie, chest mark + sleeve caption | Gildan 18500 (or Stanley Stella Cruiser 2.0 for premium) | Graphite, Bone | $84 |

Design language is in `designs/`. The flagship is the heat-hold-cool back print (`designs/heat-hold-cool.svg`). The chest mark is the wordmark plus the monospace "MADE BY A MACHINE" caption from the logo lockup.

## Why these blanks

Bella+Canvas 3001 is the proven default for fit and printability and has the lowest base on Printify's network. Comfort Colors 1717 gives the heavier, garment-dyed hand that reads premium and justifies the price. Stanley Stella is the organic-cotton option if we lean into the sustainability angle, at a higher base. Final blank gets confirmed at setup against live base costs, because the same SKU varies 10 to 20 percent across print providers.

## Sizing and variants

XS to 3XL on every SKU. Print on demand means carrying the full size range costs nothing extra, which is itself a small ethical and conversion win.

## Sequencing

1. Drop 01 (this file): three SKUs, validate that anyone buys.
2. Drop 02: only if drop 01 converts. Expand the winning silhouette, add a cap or beanie, one new design.
3. Standing line: the one or two SKUs that actually sell become always-available, the rest stay limited.

No drop 02 designs get built until drop 01 has real conversion data.
