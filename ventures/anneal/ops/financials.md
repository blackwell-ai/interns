# Unit economics and budget

All base costs are 2026 estimates from the Printify, Printful, and Gelato pricing pages and need confirming at setup, because the same blank varies 10 to 20 percent depending on which print provider fulfills it. Payment fee assumed at 2.9 percent + $0.30 (Shopify Payments / Stripe).

## Per-unit contribution

| SKU | Retail | Est. base + print | Payment fee | Contribution if customer pays shipping | Contribution if shipping is free (~$5 to $8 absorbed) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| AN-TEE-01 | $42 | ~$11 | ~$1.52 | ~$29 | ~$22 |
| AN-TEE-02 | $46 | ~$14 | ~$1.63 | ~$30 | ~$23 |
| AN-HOOD-01 | $84 | ~$26 | ~$2.74 | ~$55 | ~$47 |

These are healthy print-on-demand margins because the brand is positioned premium, not as a $20 slogan tee. The risk is conversion, not margin.

## Fixed monthly

| Item | Cost |
|---|---|
| Shopify Basic | ~$39/mo (often $1/mo for the first 3 months on promo) |
| Domain | ~$12 to $20 per year |
| Email and SMS (Klaviyo) | $0 on the free tier under 250 contacts, scales with list |
| Print on demand subscription | $0 to start (Printify free tier), Premium ~$29/mo only once volume justifies the lower base |

So the standing cost to be open for business is roughly $40 a month. There is no inventory outlay.

## What actually needs funding

Inventory risk is near zero, so the only real money is the test budget for paid acquisition plus a few sample orders to check print quality before launch.

Recommended starting envelope, which is your call:
- Samples: ~$60 to order one of each SKU and inspect quality. Worth doing.
- Paid test: a hard cap of $10 per day on one channel (Meta or TikTok), ~$300 over a first month.
- Buffer for tools and the domain: ~$100.

A $500 test budget validates whether anyone outside the founder network buys, with almost all of it recoverable as learning rather than sunk inventory. I will not spend past whatever ceiling you set, and I will not raise it without asking.

## Break-even intuition

At ~$25 average contribution per order, the ~$40 monthly fixed cost is covered by two orders. The paid-acquisition test is the real question: if blended customer acquisition cost lands under ~$25 on the tee and under ~$45 on the hoodie, the unit economics work and we scale the channel. If not, we lean on the organic founder-network and story-driven distribution and keep paid off.
