## Notes

### Ulysse Nardin: Business Overview

- 180-year-old independent watchmaker, ~9,000 watches delivered annually in North America
- Distribution is intentionally limited: 40 wholesale accounts + 2 boutiques across US and Canada
- Price range: $13,000 entry-level to $500,000+
  - Average transaction: ~$100,000
  - Top customers spend $1M+ per year across all watch brands
- Exclusivity is core to the brand: “if you don’t see us but you know about us, it’s already amazing”
- CRM on HubSpot; e-commerce exists but direct sales volume is very low

### Customer Profile

- Always existing collectors, never first-time buyers: typically own 20-30 watches already
- Actively avoid mass-market brands (Rolex, Omega, etc.); seek independent historic makers
- Geographic concentration: significant business within 100-mile radius of Stanford; Zuckerberg/Meta cited as customer example
- Best single store delivers ~20-25 units per year

### Research and Discovery Behavior

- ~85-88% of customers research online before visiting a store (Hodinkee data cited)
  - Browse brand website, watch media (Hodinkee, WatchOnista, A Blog to Watch)
  - Arrive in-store with deep technical knowledge, seeking reassurance and wrist feel
- Remaining 10-15%: wealthy buyers already in-store, asking staff “what’s new and interesting”
  - This is a key discovery channel for lesser-known independent brands

### Marketing Strategy

- No mass advertising; brand deliberately stays hard to find
- Key spend areas:
  - Collector events: intimate dinners and panels with 60+ local millionaires, billionaires, industry leaders (e.g. Park City Montage next week, secret dinner in NYC with French soccer players)
  - Watch fairs: Geneva (Watches & Wonders), Watch Time New York
  - Sponsorships: Jumbo 3000 car rally (customer overlap with Ferrari/Lamborghini owners)
  - Retail capex: store buildouts, POS materials, staff training via TopCoach mobile app
- Total US marketing budget: ~$1M (described as large for the company, small for the industry)
