## Notes

### Maison Louis Marie: Brand and Discovery

- 10-year-old clean fragrance brand, LA-based, fully bootstrapped (no investors)
- Product lines: home fragrance, perfume oils, eau de parfums, body care
- Strong D2C storefront + Amazon; just opened flagship store in Silver Lake
- Previously sold through Sephora; also in subscription beauty boxes (Ipsy, FabFitFun)
- Core discovery challenge: fragrance is high-ticket and can’t be sampled online
  - Tactics: travel sprays, 3ml roll-on minis, violin cards (sample sprayers in every box)
  - Pop-up shop in LA trialed recently; flagship store opened last week
- Pricing tiers matter: body mists for younger shoppers, ~$100 range (Maison’s zone), $300+ for DS & Durga tier

### AI and Product Discovery

- Speaker B curious but skeptical: AI could help with personalization, but risks “stealing the joy of discovery” for sensory categories like fragrance
- Sees clearer AI use cases in:
  - Resale/luxury: finding a specific item (sneakers, handbags) across all resale platforms
  - Visual search: snap a runway or TV outfit, find where to buy it or a dupe
  - Fragrance: “here are 3 scents you might like, here are 3 places to go smell them”
- Current AI landscape per Armaan:
  - LLMs mostly scrape the web; brands with unfriendly sites get poor coverage
  - Reddit, YouTube, and social proof heavily influence recommendations
  - ~50-60% of shoppers now using AI for product discovery
  - Protocols for direct brand onboarding exist but fragmented; LLM companies not prioritizing it
- Armaan’s framing: AI has the user data (memory systems), but lacks rich, structured product data
- Speaker B’s observation: AI adoption varies sharply by geography; Midwest executives largely unaware, requiring both product sell and education
