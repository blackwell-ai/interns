## Notes

### Rakuten Overview

- Marketplace since 1997, distinct from Amazon: merchants have their own storefronts
- Businesses span e-commerce, fintech, mobile, travel, banking, credit cards, golf, and more
- 120M+ members in Japan, all linked by a single universal ID
- Interviewee’s focus: Rakuten Mobile (since 2018), part of group management, not directly in e-commerce

### Loyalty and Customer Stickiness

- Points earned across every Rakuten service (shopping, credit card, travel, banking, mobile, offline)
- Points redeemable online and offline, e.g. paying mobile bills with points
- Churn near zero for members using 5+ services
- Reviews and link clicks also rewarded with points, driving UGC

### Data and Personalization

- 176 attributes per member: age, location, device, purchase history, online/offline behavior
- Cross-service data used to personalize campaigns within GDPR constraints
- If a mobile user shops on Amazon instead of Rakuten, targeted campaigns activate
- Rakuten claims more data than Amazon and Google in Japan

### Growth Strategy and AI Opportunity

- Low-CAC services (points program, golf, etc.) used as entry points to acquire new users
- Users then upsold to higher-CAC, higher-LTV services (e-commerce, credit card)
- Biggest opportunity and threat: AI shopping agents
  - If agents handle purchasing, traditional advertising loses relevance
  - Current focus: semantic search and multi-product recommendations at query time
  - No specific strategy yet for influencing AI agent recommendations


### AI Visibility Work (Note-taker’s Pitch)

- Structuring product data for LLM discoverability: llms.txt, robots.txt, invisible pages
- Tracking brand mentions across answer engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude) via automated API queries
- Sending product samples to reviewers to generate content that informs query trends
- Interviewee’s question: how to get data on what customers are actually asking AI agents
  - Note-taker acknowledged this is harder, likely lives on the brand/retailer side
