## Notes

### About Primal Souls

- Sustainable slipper brand founded in 2017 by two brothers, inspired by beach waste
- Recently transitioned from B2C to B2B focus (hotels, spas, wellness centers)
- Shifted from cork to bio-based materials: more durable, gaining traction
- 10 pilots launching in ~1.5 weeks
- Contact joined ~1 month ago; brothers run day-to-day operations

### Business Model and Value Proposition

- Core pitch: long-term cost savings over disposable slippers
  - Hotels spending ~$30K/year on disposables vs. ~$2K on reusable Primal Souls slippers
  - Higher upfront cost, but ROI over 3-5 years

- Secondary benefits: ESG reporting, guest experience alignment in luxury segment
- Revenue streams: B2B (primary momentum), retail partners, and B2C online

### Circular Supply Chain

- End-of-life return program: hotels collect used slippers, Primal Souls retrieves and grinds them back into new pairs
- ~7,000 pairs piloted in Amsterdam hotel
- Retail incentive: customers paid to return slippers at end of life
- Bio-based slippers expected to be taken home more (sturdier), but hotel-controlled returns still in place

### Marketing and Operations

- Current channels: website/blog (4-5 posts/week), LinkedIn, Instagram; TikTok under consideration
- Discovery primarily through events, speaking engagements, and word-of-mouth within hotel networks; SEO also working (ranks highly for “sustainable slippers”)
- 8 interns starting in ~1.5 weeks: will handle content and blog automation
- Authentic content gap: no hotel footage yet since pilots haven’t launched; plan to send free products for video reviews once first batch arrives
- Key sales challenge: convincing hotels to change ingrained “take-make-discard” habits

### GEO Audit and Proposal

- Primal Souls rated better than many brands audited, but gaps in recommendability across ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity
- Main technical issues identified:
  - Product reviews not embedded in page metadata
  - Missing llms.txt file (helps AI engines understand the site)
  - Various schema/metadata gaps

- Proposal: 30-day engagement, $1,000 flat fee, money-back guarantee if no improvement
- Reference clients: Public Goods and Good Molecules (~$100M+ brands)
- GEO not yet a priority for Primal Souls, but flagged as interesting by the founders when Samarjit’s team reached out
