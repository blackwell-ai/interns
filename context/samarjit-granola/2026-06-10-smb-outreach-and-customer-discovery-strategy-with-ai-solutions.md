## Notes

### Current Customer Approach

- Lead gen: Claude scrapes domain names, Apollo generates leads, mass email outreach
- Conversion flow: response → discovery call → second meeting → contract proposal
- Messaging still weak; will improve once there are live customers to reference as social proof
- Two first customer meetings today: Public Goods (beauty/DTC) and GNM Liquors (retail)
  - GNM Liquors: wants website + online ordering, decade-old inventory software, uses DoorDash integrations
  - Public Goods: geo-targeting first, then expand; CEO is a Dartmouth alum (helped with initial access)
  - Smaller DTC brands like these often have only one engineer managing Shopify and storefronts

### Target Market and Thesis

- Two main demographics: SMBs (shop owners) and DTC/e-commerce brands
- Recurring pain points surfacing: inventory management and geo-targeting
- Sample size still too small to confirm thesis; need more customers to validate
- Advisor framing: treat this like scientists with a hypothesis, test it in the real world
- DTC is a dense space: map the full connected graph (Shopify, supply chain, marketing, etc.) before going deep
- If e-commerce doesn’t pan out quickly, move on to another vertical

### Go-to-Market Strategy

- Top priority: go live with current customers AND keep outreaching simultaneously
- Goal: 10 customers live by next week, problems actually solved (not just signed up)
- In-person outreach strongly recommended, especially in the Bay Area
  - Mission District (SF) has many small businesses; skate shop owner today was a strong example
  - Being in-person builds relationships, not just transactions; likability is a key sales lever
  - Younger, more open-minded owners convert faster and are more receptive to AI
- Don’t lead with AI; shadow customers, watch what they do, find the real problems
- Other idea-sourcing channels: Craigslist odd jobs, Fiverr/Upwork job postings (how geo was discovered)
- Avoid leaning on YC brand for inbound right now
  - YC is a multiplier, not a fire-starter; need to build outreach muscle independently

### Weekly Rhythm and Goals

- Week-by-week goals preferred over long-term planning at this stage
- Suggested daily split: customer conversations by day, software building by night
- Come back next week with learnings from 10 live customers

### Next Steps

- Go live with existing customers (Public Goods, GNM Liquors, and the new one Ethan found)
- Hit 10 live customers by next week, problems fully solved
- Prioritize in-person outreach in the Bay Area (San Jose now, SF soon)
- Map the full DTC ecosystem to identify surgical entry points
- Check Craigslist, Fiverr, and Upwork for problem signals and potential customer types
- Stay in closer weekly contact with advisor to review learnings
